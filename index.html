A Circular Story Built in Arba Minch

Based in the vibrant agricultural paradise of Arba Minch, Ethiopia, Sewasew bridges the beautiful gap between historic manual craftsmanship and global environmental stewardship.

We collect natural banana pseudostem fibers—an abundant agricultural byproduct usually burned or left to waste after harvests—and convert them into extremely tough, versatile, natural packaging sheets and bags.

“Our name, Sewasew, embodies the perfect connection and ladder of progress between community development, ethical employment, and natural cycles.”

Our chemical-free formulation ensures that our paper returns safely back to the earth, composting completely within a matter of weeks and replenishing local soil ecosystems without microplastics or synthetic toxins.

Frequently Asked Questions

Because Sewasew paper products are strictly 100% natural banana fiber and avoid synthetic chemical lamination or plastic finishes, they break down naturally in standard home composting arrays in just 6 to 12 weeks. They will safely decompose in any regular garden or open soil framework, adding vital organic nutrients.

Banana pseudostem fiber fibers are among the strongest natural fibers in the world, with incredible cellular tensile strength. In contrast to wood pulp, which requires massive chemical intervention and mechanical shearing that shortens fibers, our manual extraction preserves the long, durable strands, translating into premium structural capability.

We offer customizable eco-conscious print branding services. We utilize organic, soy-based, and botanical water inks to ensure your company logo is displayed cleanly while strictly maintaining our plastic-free, circular composting values.
